### Idea 3: "Urban Explorer's Field Guide"
**Design Movement**: Swiss Style / International Typographic Style
**Core Principles**:
1.  **Functionality First**: Information is king; design serves the content.
2.  **Bold Typography**: Large, impactful type for hierarchy.
3.  **Structured Chaos**: Rigid grids broken by dynamic image placement.
4.  **Vibrant Utility**: Using color to code information (e.g., price vs. speed).

**Color Philosophy**:
-   **Background**: Cool light gray (`#f0f2f5`) or crisp white.
-   **Accents**: International Orange (`#ff4f00`), Cobalt Blue (`#0047ab`), and Signal Green (`#00a651`).
-   **Intent**: Feel like a high-end travel magazine or a well-designed transit map. Efficient, trustworthy, and cool.

**Layout Paradigm**:
-   **Modular Grid**: Blocks of content that fit together like a puzzle.
-   **Split Screen**: Text on one side, sticky visuals on the other.
-   **Horizontal Scroll**: Sections that scroll sideways for comparison tables.

**Signature Elements**:
-   **Thick Dividers**: Bold lines separating sections.
-   **Oversized Numbers**: Big typography for stats (prices, battery life).
-   **Cut-Out Images**: Photos of bikes/scooters with backgrounds removed, interacting with the text.

**Interaction Philosophy**:
-   **Direct & Responsive**: Immediate response to clicks.
-   **Sticky Headers**: Navigation and key info stay visible.

**Animation**:
-   **Slide-Ups**: Content blocks slide up into place.
-   **Parallax Images**: Images move slightly faster/slower than text.

**Typography System**:
-   **Headings**: **Helvetica Now** or **Inter** (Bold, tight tracking).
-   **Body**: **Roboto** or **Public Sans** (Neutral, highly readable).

## Selected Approach: "Urban Explorer's Field Guide" (Idea 3)

**Reasoning**: This approach strikes the best balance between the user's need for clear, comparable information (pricing, rules, how-to) and the desire for a "modern, professional yet approachable" design. It allows for excellent data visualization (one of the user's key requests) while maintaining a stylish, magazine-like aesthetic that fits travel content. The "Neon Tokyo" idea might be too dark/niche for a general guide, and "Zen" might feel too slow for someone looking for quick transit info. "Urban Explorer" is practical, bold, and scalable.

**Design Philosophy Statement**:
"We will adopt the **'Urban Explorer's Field Guide'** aesthetic, inspired by Swiss Style design. This means prioritizing **bold typography**, a **modular grid layout**, and **vibrant utility colors** to create a highly functional yet visually striking interface. The design will feel like a premium interactive travel magazine, using **oversized numbers** for data visualization and **cut-out imagery** to make the vehicles pop. Our goal is to make the complex information about bike sharing instantly digestible and shareable, fulfilling the user's request for intuitive data exploration."
